Low-cost Vision Based 3D laser scanner for 3D reconstruction

Designed a low-cost Vision based 3D laser scanner for 3D reconstruction. Developer, Research advisor: Prof. Huijun Gao.

..

Flow Chart


Highlights

  • The hardware comprises of a line laser transmitter, a web camera and three calibration boards.
  • Two key problems have been solved: 1) camera calibration, Zhang’s method is applied to calibrate the camera with lens tangential and radial distortions are also considered; 2) laser stripe center extraction, Hessian matrix is employed to extract the sub-pixel center position line of the laser stripe.
  • Results show high precision in reconstruction results. We assume if both camera and calibration boards are fixed, this device provides great flexibility as camera calibration is only needed once and users just use the laser transmitter to scan up and down. Therefore this 3D laser scanner can be greatly welcomed for its low cost and convenience.
