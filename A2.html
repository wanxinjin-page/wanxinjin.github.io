Ball Grid Array (BGA) component recognition and inspection

Designed two types of Ball Grid Array (BGA) chip identification and inspection algorithms for Surface Mounting Machines. Researcher, Research advisor: Huijun Gao, Intelligent Control and System Lab at HIT.


Highlights

  • In first method, our contributions are: 1) propose an adaptive thresholding combined with modified (epsilon,delta)-component segmentation to maximize the extraction integrity of solder balls under inhomogeneous image brightness. 2) Propose a reference-free line-based-clustering method to identify ball array, simultaneously obtaining the position and orientation angle of BGA with respect to image coordinate system.
  • In second, we first introduce an adaptive thresholding and a median-based Otsu method to separately determine an optimal threshold for segmenting each ball. Then, template-free pathing-walking mechanism is proposed to recognize the locations of the balls in the grid. Based on the recognition result, BGA position and orientation are computed. Simultaneously, feature extraction and defect detection are performed.
  • Practical applications show that our algorithms are suitable for a vast majority of BGAs with different ball alignments, and are also robust to interferences caused by segmentations. Results further indicates that compared with the Samsung’s algorithm, our approach exhibits higher computational efficiency and promising inspection accuracy even under unsatisfying illumination conditions.
  • Produced two highly-cited journal papers (IEEE Transactions on Industrial Electronics, IF:6.383) and one international patent through PCT.
