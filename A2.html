Ball Grid Array (BGA) component recognition and inspection

Designed two types of algorithms for Ball Grid Array (BGA) component recognition and inspection. Intelligent Control and System Lab at HIT, Postgraduate researcher, Research advisor: Huijun Gao.


Highlights

  • In these two algorithms, solder ball adaptive segmentation based on gray scale is proposed, which can avoid over-segmentation or under-segmentation of solder ball under inconsistency of image brightness.
  • In first BGA recognition algorithm, path-walking method is proposed to identify the relative position of each solder ball in the array. In the second algorithm, line-based-clustering approach is proposed to classify each solder ball into corresponding row ball set and column ball set. Both algorithms are tactfully designed to avoid time-consuming matching process utilized by classical algorithms.
  • In practical implementation, it shows that our algorithm is suitable for a vast majority of BGAs with different ball arrays and also robust to interferences caused by segmentations. Correct detection rates under some serious light conditions and the time efficiency outperform the algorithm adopted in SMT 482 (Samsung). The processing time of the proposed algorithm is less than 200ms for BGA components with ball number below 500.
  • The project has produced two high-level papers which are excepted to be published in IEEE Transactions on Mechatronics and IEEE Transactions on Industrial Electronics. This project has also produced three national patents which have been submitted and are under substantive examination. Additionally, one algorithm is now being applied for international patent through PTC.
