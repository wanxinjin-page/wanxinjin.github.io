Ball Grid Array (BGA) component recognition and inspection

Designed two types of Ball Grid Array (BGA) inspection algorithms for Surface Mounting Technology. Intelligent Control and System Lab at HIT, Research advisor: Huijun Gao.


Highlights

  • In these two algorithms, adaptive segmentation method is proposed for extracting gray-scale solder balls from the original captured image. This method can avoid over-segmentation or under-segmentation of solder ball under inconsistency of illuminations.
  • In first BGA recognition algorithm, a path-walking method is proposed to identify the relative position of each solder ball in the array. In the second algorithm, a line-based-clustering approach is proposed to classify each solder ball into the corresponding row ball set and column ball set. Both algorithms are tactfully designed to avoid time-consuming matching process of classical algorithms.
  • In practical implementation, it shows that our algorithm is suitable for a vast majority of BGAs with different ball arrays and also robust to interferences caused by segmentations. Correct detection rates under some serious light conditions as well as time efficiency outperform the algorithm adopted in SMT 482 (Samsung). The processing time of the proposed algorithm is less than 200ms for BGA components with ball number below 500.
  • The project has produced two high-level papers which are excepted to be published in IEEE Transactions on Industrial Electronics. This project has also produced three national patents. Additionally, one algorithm is now being applied for international patent through PTC.
