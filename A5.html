Real-time digital image stabilization (DIS) algorithm

Design a real-time digital image stabilization (DIS) algorithm for mobile phones. Core developer, project advisor: Prof. Xiaoguang Di, Control and Simulation Center at HIT, Project designer: Huawei Technologies Co. Ltd.

Highlights

  • DIS algorithm consists of three stages: image motion estimation to estimate the inter-frame translation and rotation motion, motion filtering to determine the unwanted motions, and image compensation to transform each frame to a smooth frame motion trajectory.
  • Image motion estimation is most critical. In our approach, a new inter-frame motion model is proposed, and a novel method based on image bit-plane pyramid is presented to estimate inter-frame rotation and translation. Wherein an adaptive-block-selected matching mechanism is proposed to handle the moving objects. Kalman filtering is employed in the motion filtering due to its better real-time performance, compared with Bezier fitting and mean filtering method.
  • Practical applications demonstrates the proposed DIS algorithm’s great performance when tackling large-scale vibrations, and the processing speed for 640*480 frame can reach 33fps.
  • The modified version of this DIS algorithm for high reliability was also adopted by Huawei Company for their next-generation mobile phones.
  • Applied for two national patents(both granted) and produced two papers.
